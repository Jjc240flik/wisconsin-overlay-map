UNFINISHED BUSINESS FOR DISCUSSION & POSSIBLE ACTION
5.a

Discuss/Consider Sub-Area Planning - Sub-Area A.
Memo - Sub Area A Plan.pdf
Sub Area A - DRAFT PLAN.pdf
Development would consist of generally single-family lots with certain areas
where commercial or multi-family residential would be appropriate. Grid design
would eliminate cul-de-sacs, lots adjacent to woodland areas would be a 1-acre
minimum, and those adjacent to streams, ½-acre minimum. Setbacks along
corridor roads to match, or average, existing developments on either side.
Road mapping – move Julius Drive to the east to CTH JJ, minimally impacting
the wooded area and maintaining park area, extend Otis Drive to STH 76 and
Morning Glory Lane to Hillview Road.
The commission discussed additional safe routes to school, pedestrian trails along
collector and main subdivision roads, along Julius Drive, Hillview Road, CTH JJ,
and Glen Valley pond to Hillview Rd., potentially extending the Glen Valley trail
to the west and sidewalks on both sides of new subdivision roads.

5.b

Discuss/Consider Sub-Area Planning - Sub-Area B.
Memo - Sub Area B Plan.pdf
Sub Area B - DRAFT PLAN.pdf
Sub Area B to be developed generally with single-family lots, however, there are
areas that may be appropriate for commercial or multiple-family residential
development (areas south of Hillview Road extended or along CTH JJ).
Utilizing ground elevations, the following were identified: a. Less than 800 -feet –
no permits or filling/grading allowed b. Between 800-820 -feet – buffer lot sizes,
no subdivisions allowed, only divisions by CSM c. Between 820-835 -feet – 1acre lot size minimum d. Between 835-850 -feet – ½-acre lot size minimum e.
Beyond 850 -feet – standard residential lots allowed (13,500-sq ft)
Adjacent to woodland areas, 1-acre lot size minimum. Setbacks along corridor
roads to match, or average, existing developments on either side.
Pedestrian trails should be constructed along collector roads and main subdivision
roads. Sidewalks on both sides of new subdivision roads. Pedestrian trail from
North Greenville Elementary School to the south.

5.c

Discuss/Consider Sub-Area Planning - Sub-Area C.
Memo - Sub Area C Plan.pdf
Sub Area C - DRAFT PLAN.pdf
Sub Area C to be developed with single-family lots. Utilizing ground elevations,
the following were identified: a. Less than 800 -feet – no permits or
filling/grading allowed b. Between 800-820 -feet – buffer lot sizes, no
subdivisions allowed, only divisions by CSM c. Between 820-835 -feet – 1-acre
lot size minimum d. Between 835-850 -feet – ½-acre lot size minimum. Beyond
850 -feet – ½-acre lot size minimum. Setbacks along corridor roads to match, or
average, existing developments on either side. There are to be transition lots
adjacent to existing development, except the 5-acre property on Everglade Road
and the 2 flag lot parcels on Mayflower Drive.
Pedestrian trails should be constructed along collector roads and main subdivision
roads. Sidewalks on both sides of new subdivision roads are optional (will still
require variance/approval from Village Board).

5.d

Discuss/Consider Comprehensive Plan Policies/Goals/Action Items - Chapter
4 Land Use
Memo - Chapter 4 Land Use.pdf
Options for Strategy 4a-5 (Greenville should consider not supporting the
development of more than two residential developments at any one time unless the
active developments have less than 5% of remaining lots available for building).
Discussed options included setting a number of lots per year or a number of
subdivisions. The section should be codified; options will be brought back for
future discussion.

Community Concerns Regarding Proposed Concept Plan – near
School Road and Julius Drive
Good evening,
I’m speaking on behalf of about 45 residents in our neighborhood, which is located on the
northern border of the proposed development being considered on School Road and Julius
Drive. Our neighborhood is comprised entirely of single-family homes on lots of at least 0.5
acres, and we believe the existing infrastructure was never designed to support highdensity subdivisions and multi-family residential units. While growth is important, we
believe this location is not an appropriate or safe place for the submitted concept plan.
We are asking for this proposal to be significantly modified from its current design to
preserve our rural character, avoid large apartment-style density at this location, and fully
address traffic and safety impacts on School Road and Julius Drive.
Below are the primary concerns we ask the Village Planning Commission and Village Board
to consider:

1. Maintain Rural Character
This neighborhood is part of Greenville’s rural edge, where people choose to live for open
space, quiet, and low-density homes. Allowing a large, urban-style project here would
permanently change that character for everyone who already lives along School Road and
Julius Drive.
Greenville’s own 2040 Comprehensive Plan, stemming from a 2018 study, states its
purpose is to guide land use decisions “while ensuring future sustainability of local natural
resources and the agricultural heritage of Greenville.” In that adopted plan, this corridor is
designated for “Suburban Residential – Tier 1”, not high-density multi-family use.

The Village’s own Comprehensive Plan and Land Use Map recognize this area as part of
Greenville’s rural, low-density fabric, not as an urban, high-density corridor. When you
override that map for an isolated project, it undermines years of planning work and public
input that shaped where development belongs. Residents who relied on those plans and
maps when they bought and improved their homes, lose trust in the planning and zoning
process.
We are asking you to uphold your Comprehensive Plan and Future Land Use Map by
keeping this corridor rural and low-density. Directing higher-density growth to areas
already planned “Urban Core” along state highways 15, 76, and 96.

___________________________________________________________________________
2. Current Roadway Infrastructure Cannot Support the Increased Traffic
The roads surrounding this proposed development — particularly School Road and Julius
Drive — are already operating at or near capacity. These roads were originally designed for
low-density residential neighborhoods, not multi-story apartments or large-scale
townhome complexes. Without serious safety upgrades, these roads will be more
dangerous for children and more frustrating for existing residents. Adding hundreds of
additional daily vehicle trips will worsen:
•
•
•
•

Congestion
Backups during peak hours at the adjacent school
Emergency vehicle response times
Risk of collisions

The state has already committed a multi-million dollar project to build a roundabout at the
WIS 76 and School Road to address known safety and traffic issues at that intersection.

The state would not have invested in that major safety project if this corridor didn’t already
have significant traffic and safety concerns. The section of School Road in this area is
essentially two travel lanes with very narrow, deep drop-offs or gravel shoulders, and the
Village is already focusing on connecting trails for our community to better accommodate
pedestrians and bicyclists. A narrow two-land road with minimal shoulders was never
designed for heavy turning movements in and out of large apartment and townhome
complexes. Adding hundreds of new daily trips and turning movements on this busy school
and church corridor without full traffic and safety analysis risks creating more safety
concerns and undermines past and future taxpayer investment.
These roads may not be able to be widened without significant disruption to existing
homes, residents, and Immanuel church, school, and cemetery. Before any approval, we
request an independent, traffic and safety study covering School Road and Julius Drive
intersection, and the developer should be required to fund the turn lanes, access changes,
and safety measures that the study identifies. Protecting children and families who already
rely on these roads must come before adding more traffic to them.

3. Severe Safety Risks for Church, School, and Children
Immanuel Lutheran Church has approximately 2,500 community members and holds
multiple services on its campus directly across from this proposed development.
Immanuel Lutheran School, on the same campus, serves about 375 students in grades
Pre-K through 8th. This proposed development sits directly in the traffic flow of the church,
daycare, and school that already struggle with high-volume traffic during drop-off and
pickup times, as well as after-school activities.
Safety risks that will be exacerbated include:
•
•
•
•

Children crossing roads
Vehicle line back-ups dangerously near entrances
Increased likelihood of pedestrian incidents
Driver frustration and hurried maneuvers during peak times

We already see safety concerns daily — adding hundreds of vehicles will significantly
increase the danger to schoolchildren. We’re asking you to say no to high-density lots,
apartments and townhomes at this location and instead require single-family, ruralfriendly designs. That still allow growth, but it keeps this corridor safe and workable for the
church, school, and existing residents.

4. Field of Dreams, Julius Drive, and School Road Cannot Handle a Major Influx

With proposed Field of Dreams, Julius Drive, and School Road all funneling into this same
corridor, the system is already stressed. Any major housing density increase would create
a bottleneck the infrastructure simply cannot absorb.
This is not a matter of inconvenience — it is a public safety issue.

5. Better and More Appropriate Locations Exist
If multi-family expansion is part of the village’s development strategy, there are
significantly better locations, such as Highways 15, 76, and 96 corridors. These areas:
•
•
•
•
•

Already have commercial-grade road infrastructure
Provide direct access to major arteries
Can support higher traffic volumes
Align more naturally with higher-density zoning
Avoid creating safety risks around schools, churches, and residential
neighborhoods

Smart growth is about placing development where the infrastructure can support it — this
is not that location.

6. Long-Term Liability and Risk for the Village
If the Board approves a development in an unsuitable location, it assumes:
•
•
•
•

Liability for foreseeable safety risks to children and residents
Potential cost for future road expansion and additional infrastructure.
Community dissatisfaction
The burden of addressing school and church traffic issues that will worsen
dramatically

Preventing a known risk is far more responsible than trying to fix it later.

7. Other Additional Factors/Concerns
Our neighborhood community also wanted to include a couple other factors and concerns
related to the property and proposal. Many residents have lived here for the last 13 years

and have photograph documentation of the field flooding each spring and fall near the
existing wetland property within our neighborhood. The current property owners have
repeatedly tried to implement various mitigation strategies throughout those years in order
to maintain usable farmland. Some of our residents were informed that the field may
contain drain tile in portions of the property. What impact does existing or old drain tile
have with this proposal? We request that the Village take consideration of this
wetland/flooding into the proposal with all proper DNR and engineering requirements for
the development.
One last concern, we request that the Village require transitional lots with existing
properties on Midnight Way.
____________________________________________________________________________________

Closing
In closing, we understand the importance of growth and housing availability. We are not
opposed to development — we are opposed to placing the wrong type of development in
the wrong location. For the safety of our children, the integrity of our neighborhood, and the
sustainability of our infrastructure, we respectfully ask the Village Board to deny this
concept plan.
We strongly urge the Village Board to consider more appropriate locations for multi-family
development such as the Hwy 15/76/96 corridor that can properly support future growth
without jeopardizing public safety and losing the green in Greenville.
Thank you for your time and consideration.

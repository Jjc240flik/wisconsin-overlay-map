### Airport Rezoning (AGD to AIR) + Sewer Service Area
**Status: APPROVED — but Village Board initially DENIED, then RECONSIDERED**

| Step | Body | Date | Vote |
|------|------|:----:|:----:|
| Rezoning PC | Plan Commission | Jan 12, 2026 | **7-0** |
| SSA Amendment VB | Village Board | Dec 8, 2025 | **3-2 DENIED** (Brian Mulroy, Andy Peters, Dean Culbertson?) |
| SSA Amendment Reconsidered | Village Board | Dec 15, 2025 | **4-0 APPROVED** |

**Key observations — THIS IS CRITICAL:**
- The Planning Commission approved unanimously (7-0) — standard
- The Village Board **voted 3-2 to DENY** a routine sewer service area amendment for the airport
- The vote was **split**: Brian Mulroy voted no, and at least one of Andy Peters/Dean Culbertson also voted no
- The next week (Dec 15), the board **reconsidered** and approved 4-0 after additional information was provided
- This shows: **the board can be persuaded with more information**

**Our takeaway:** A routine use (airport) was initially denied on a split vote. If a routine airport SSA can get a 3-2 denial, our subdivision will face scrutiny. But the fact they reconsidered and approved shows the board responds to better information.

## 2. CONTENTIOUS PROPOSALS (Heated Opposition)

### School Road / Julius Drive Concept Plan
**Status: IN PROCESS — facing organized, vocal opposition**

**Opposition organized:**
- A **petition of ~45 residents** was presented at the Dec 8, 2025 PC meeting
- Residents read a prepared statement citing:
  - "Rural character" preservation
  - Traffic and safety concerns
  - School capacity
  - Density (apartments / multi-family)
  - Water runoff/drainage
  - Inconsistency with the 2040 Comprehensive Plan designation
- Multiple meetings of public comment from: John Julius, Jean Goffard, Linda Kester, Bruce Bennett, Bob Glidden, Rev. Eric Roth, Kim Johnson, and others

**Project details:**
- Concept plan for parcels 111063600 & 111063502 (near School Road and Julius Drive)
- Developer: Ebbens (Ted Ebben, Bob Ebben)
- Discussed across 5+ PC meetings (Oct 2025 through May 2026)
- Involved transitional lots, 0.5-acre lot sizes, vegetation buffer, 55+ housing
- Traffic study required, park land required

**Our takeaway:** This is the **most organized opposition** in the record. 45 residents coordinated a petition and speaking campaign. The developer is still working through the process — it hasn't been denied, but it's taking much longer than Savannah Heights.

### The Positives

| Factor | Precedent |

| AGD to residential rezoning is standard | Harvest Ridge, Airport — both approved |
| Concept plan process works | Every developer uses it |
| PC is pro-development | Jack Anderson and Leanne Meidam-Wincentsen lead consistently |

| Village staff supports Sub-Area C development | Mark Mommaerts confirmed this |
| The property is in Sub-Area Plan C (adopted) | The plan explicitly anticipates residential development |

### The Challenges

| Challenge | Source | Severity |
|-----------|--------|:--------:|
| **Sandra Lenz (Everglade Rd)** | Vocal opponent for 18+ months, knows the process | HIGH |
| **Everglade Road neighbors** | Organized, attend meetings, speak consistently | MODERATE |
| **Lift station cost/controversy** | Dean Culbertson raised concerns; adds cost | MODERATE |
| **Board skepticism** | Brian Mulroy, Dean Culbertson voted no on routine airport SSA | MODERATE |
| **Sub-Area C history** | Created after the Skiba denial — larger lot sizes are political compromise | LOW |
| **Board overruled PC before** | Doug's Whispering Winds was approved by PC, blocked by VB | LOW |

### The Strategy That Works (Based on These Findings)

1. **Work with Doug Gunderson** — He has the relationship with the board. The July 13 meeting showed momentum. Aligning with him gives us political cover.

2. **Preempt the opposition** — Sandra Lenz and the Everglade neighbors will speak against us. Address their concerns (traffic, drainage, school capacity) in the concept plan BEFORE the meeting. Present a traffic study upfront. Offer a drainage solution pre-packaged.

3. **Court Jack Anderson first** — He's on both PC and VB, makes almost every motion. If he's on board, the project has momentum.

4. **Identify Brian Mulroy and Dean Culbertson's specific concerns** — They're the skeptical votes. Doug's lift station discussion was the right approach — engage them directly on their stated concerns.

5. **Follow the Savannah Heights playbook, not the Skiba playbook** — Savannah Heights got through because the developer was experienced and worked within the rules. Skiba got denied because neighbors organized against density. Our lot sizes (0.5-1 acre, per Sub-Area C) already address the density objection.

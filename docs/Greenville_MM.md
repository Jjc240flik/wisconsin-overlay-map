## 5. KEY POLITICAL INTELLIGENCE FROM MARK MOMMAERTS (PLANNER)

### Meeting Summary — July 2026

**Mark's Role:** Planning staff — supports development but defers to board decisions

**Critical Historical Context:**
- **Dustin Skiba** submitted a subdivision proposal a couple years ago
- Neighbors raised concerns about **drainage and traffic** — typical NIMBY issues
- The subdivision was **denied** and the village created the **Sub-Area Plan C** as a compromise
- Sub-Area Plan C established **larger lot sizes** (1+ acre minimums in some areas)

**Lot Size Constraints by Area:**
1. **Western area (CSM only):** Maximum **4 lots**, minimum **2-3 acres** per lot
   - Park, pond, and lift station can be located here
   - These 4 lots are the maximum for this entire area — cannot be increased
2. **Middle area (1 acre min):** Lots at **1 acre minimum** — could potentially be negotiated to 0.75-0.8 acres
3. **Eastern area (0.5 acre min):** Lots at **0.5 acre minimum**

**Staff Position:**
- Supports development of this parcel
- Village has proactively sized water and sewer infrastructure for this area
- Staff follows adopted Sub-Area Plan concepts and expects developers to work within them
- Staff is NOT the decision-maker — the Village Board holds final authority

**Mark's Advice for the Process:**
- *"It doesn't hurt to ask"* — regarding proposing slightly different lot sizes
- Submit a **concept plan** ($200-250 fee) to get formal feedback
- Be **open to feedback** — developers who come in demanding their way rub the commission wrong
- Work through the concept plan process iteratively — it's designed for compromise

**Key Quotes:**
- *"I'm always of the thought that it doesn't hurt to ask"* — regarding proposing different lot sizes
- *"Staff wise, yeah, I think we would [like to see this developed]. We have water plans to service this area. We've sized some downstream interceptors to serve this area."*
- *"We understand that higher density is a return on our investments and helps spread costs over more properties."*

## 6. KEY INTELLIGENCE FROM DOUG GUNDERSON (ADJACENT DEVELOPER)

### Phone Call Summary — July 2026

**Doug's Status:**
- **2 years** navigating the Village of Greenville approval process
- Has completed: surveys, wetlands study, geotechnical, concept plans, traffic study
- **Planning Commission approved** his original plan after 3 months of revisions
- **Village Board rejected** it — wanted larger lots, no stated code justification
- Doug **sued the village** (court hasn't ruled) — his attorney recommended approaching the board again
- At the **July 13, 2026 Village Board meeting**, Doug felt momentum shifting in his favor

**Key Insights for Our Strategy:**

1. **The board is the obstacle** — not staff, not the planning commission
   - Planning commission approved Doug's plan because it met the code
   - Board rejected it for reasons that may not have been code-compliant
   - Doug's lawsuit (the two state laws he referenced) may pressure the board to act more consistently

2. **Lot size expectations** — the board wants larger lots, but:
   - The subdivision to the south (across the street) has **0.3-0.5 acre lots**
   - Those were built **15 years ago** with no complaints
   - The current Sub-Area Plan C already mandates larger lots in certain areas
   - There may be room to negotiate between the 1-acre minimum and the 0.5-acre minimum

3. **The lift station is the key infrastructure piece**
   - Doug proposed placing it on the 63-acre parcel (lower elevation)
   - Village utility department head supports the location
   - This makes more sense than the village's planned diagonal route
   - **Coordination with Doug on this is essential** — shared cost, better location

4. **Demand is confirmed**
   - **Savannah Heights:** 35 lots sold out in 60 days, now expanding
   - **Cypress Homes (Grand Chute):** 1-acre lots selling at ~$150,000
   - **Builder buying at $110K for 0.75 acre lot** and reselling for $134.9K (under contract)
   - *"Greenville's a high demand area... it's only like a mile and a half from US 41, the location's good."*

5. **Doug's willingness to work together**
   - He previously made an offer on the 63 acres himself
   - He proposed joint park, joint pond, joint lift station
   - He's open to coordinating with us — *"You can call me anytime"*
   - His timeline: expects resolution on his project in the next "couple months"

## 7. VILLAGE BOARD POLITICAL LANDSCAPE — PRELIMINARY ASSESSMENT

### Structure

| Body | Role | Key Person | Notes |
|------|------|------------|-------|
| **Village Board** | Final approval authority | Village President (overlaps with PC) | Doug's rejection came from here |
| **Planning Commission** | Recommendations to Board | President of Village also serves | Approved Doug's plan; board overruled |
| **Staff** | Technical review + recommendations | Mark Mommaerts (Planner) | Supports development; not decision-maker |
| **Utility Department** | Infrastructure feasibility | Department head | Supports Doug's lift station location |

### What to Watch For

1. **Village Board voting patterns** — Read minutes. Is it a 4-3 split or unanimous? Are certain board members consistently anti-development?

2. **Public opposition patterns** — The Skiba denial was driven by neighbor complaints about drainage and traffic. Are there organized opponents? What specific concerns do they raise?

3. **The "Wisconsin Laws" (Laws 64 and 25)** — Doug referenced two state laws that require municipalities to follow existing zoning codes after a preliminary plan is submitted. If the board rejected Doug's plan despite it meeting code, there may be legal vulnerability. Success of Doug's lawsuit could set a precedent favorable to us.

4. **Recent shift in momentum** — Doug said after the July 13, 2026 meeting he "felt a lot better than I had in a long time." What changed? Identify which board members may have shifted position.

### Recommended Reading Order for Minutes

1. **July 13, 2026 Village Board** — Doug presented; utility department head supported lift station plan
2. **November 10, 2025 Village Board** — 6+ hour meeting — likely the most substantive
3. **Any meeting referencing "Dustin Skiba"** — understand the denial that created Sub-Area Plan C
4. **Any meeting referencing "Whispering Winds"** — Doug's project name? Look for this keyword
5. **Planning Commission meetings** — for technical details on lot sizes, densities, engineering requirements

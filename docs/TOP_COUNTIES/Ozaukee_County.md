# Ozaukee County – Future Property Development Vision
## TOP COUNTY – Priority Land Research Target

## General Breakdown: Ozaukee County's Vision for Future Property Development

Ozaukee County is part of the Milwaukee metro north shore. Growth is driven by Milwaukee commuters seeking suburban and exurban lifestyles. Development is concentrated along the I-43 corridor.

### Key Growth Corridors
- I-43 corridor (Milwaukee → Mequon → Cedarburg → Grafton → Port Washington → Saukville)
- Highway 60 corridor (west-east connection)
- Lake Michigan shoreline (high-value properties)

### Key Priorities
| Priority | What Planners Want |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Focus growth in areas with sewer/water |
| Lake Michigan Preservation | Protect bluffs, shoreline, and water quality |
| Housing Variety | Mix of housing types |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect farmland in western townships |

**Bottom Line**
Highest subdivision potential is in the southern edge (I-43 corridor near Milwaukee) and the villages and towns along the I-43 growth corridor — Cedarburg, Grafton, Port Washington, Saukville. Western towns are more agricultural.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas (Priority 1):**
Mequon (City), Grafton (Village), Saukville (Village), Town of Cedarburg — I-43 corridor and Milwaukee metro fringe.

**Moderate to High:**
Cedarburg (City), Town of Grafton — Edge growth near cities.

**Low Growth:**
Western towns (Holland, Fredonia Town, Belgium Town) — Agricultural and rural. Lower development pressure.

# Waukesha County – Future Property Development Vision
## TOP COUNTY – Priority Land Research Target

## General Breakdown: Waukesha County's Vision for Future Property Development

Waukesha County is one of Wisconsin's fastest-growing counties, driven by Milwaukee metro expansion. Planners want controlled growth that preserves the county's lake country character while accommodating development pressure.

### Key Growth Corridors
- I-94 corridor (Milwaukee → Waukesha → Brookfield → Pewaukee)
- US 18 corridor
- Highway 83 corridor

### 1. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Waukesha County's growth is centered on the I-94 corridor, with the strongest pressure in villages and towns on the eastern edge (closer to Milwaukee).

### 2. Western Lakes Country = Lower Growth
The western portion of the county (Town of Delafield, Oconomowoc, Summit, etc.) has more limited development due to lake country preservation and seasonal tourism.

### 3. Key Priorities
 | Priority | What Planners Want |
|---|---|
 | Infrastructure Efficiency | Develop where sewer/water exist |
 | Lake Country Preservation | Protect lakes, wetlands, and natural areas |
 | Housing Variety | Mix of housing types |
 | Economic Growth | Support commercial corridors along I-94 |

**Bottom Line**
Highest subdivision potential is in the eastern edge of the county — villages and towns on the Milwaukee metro fringe where the strongest farmland-to-residential transition is occurring.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas (Priority 1):**
Menomonee Falls, Lisbon, Genesee, Pewaukee (Village), Sussex, Town of Waukesha, Town of Brookfield — I-94 corridor and Milwaukee metro fringe.

**Moderate to High:**
Lannon, Muskego, Merton, Vernon — Edge-adjacent growth.

**Low Growth:**
Western towns (Eagle, Ottawa, Summit, Delafield) — Lake country preservation and less development pressure.

# Calumet County Future Growth Direction — Research Report
**Generated: 2026-07-12 | Sources: 2045 Draft Comp Plan, FLU Map, County Parcel Data**

---

## Executive Summary

Calumet County's growth is **bifurcated into two distinct dynamics**: (1) Appleton/Fox Cities suburban spillover in the northwest, and (2) Lake Winnebago shoreline seasonal/amenity development in the east. The 2045 Comprehensive Plan (still in draft; adoption expected Summer 2025) explicitly directs growth to existing developed areas while aggressively protecting farmland. The FLU map was released Jan–Feb 2025 and uses new mixed-use categories.

---

## Is Growth Driven by Appleton's Southward Expansion?

**Yes, overwhelmingly so.** This is the dominant growth engine:

- **Village of Harrison accounts for over 50% of all county population growth** since 2000. It doubled from ~5,700 (2000) to ~12,500 (2020).
- The 2045 plan projects the largest future growth in Harrison, Menasha, and Appleton (Calumet portions) — all northwest corridor.
- **Why:** "Proximity of jobs within Winnebago and Outagamie counties, and the appeal of settling in a suburban environment" (Chapter 1, Demographics).
- **79.5%** of Calumet County workers commute OUT of the county, primarily to the Fox Cities (Appleton/Oshkosh/Neenah). The county functions as a bedroom community for the Fox Cities metro.
- The Appleton metro's urbanized area now extends into Calumet County via the US 10/US 41/CTH KK corridors.
- **Household income pattern confirms it:** Northwest jurisdictions (in Appleton urban area) have significantly higher median incomes than the rural rest of the county (e.g., Sherwood $125K vs. Stockbridge $57K).

---

## Is the Lake Winnebago Shoreline (Stockbridge, Brothertown) a Different Dynamic?

**Yes — separate and distinct from the Fox Cities sprawl:**

- Stockbridge and Brothertown are **NOT governed by the County 2045 Comp Plan** — they have their own separate comprehensive plans. The County plan covers only: Towns of Brillion, Brothertown, Charlestown, Chilton, New Holstein, Rantoul, Woodville; City of Brillion; Villages of Hilbert and Potter.
- **Stockbridge (Town)** has the highest concentration of B+ rezoning-potential parcels (203 parcels) in the county data, but these are mostly small shoreline lots (~4 acres avg). This is **infill/seasonal/second-home development**, not large-scale subdivision.
- **Brothertown (Town)** has 176 B+ parcels, similar pattern.
- The lakeshore dynamic is **amenity-driven**: seasonal homes, vacation properties, retirees. The comp plan explicitly notes that seasonal/second homes around Lake Winnebago "do not serve as permanent housing for households."
- Development constraints: Niagara Escarpment (25-ft limestone cliff), wetlands, floodplains, and Brothertown's active quarry limit (200 acres max). These areas are marked for environmental protection, not large-scale growth.
- The lakeshore communities face lower incomes, limited services, and lack the employment pull that drives the northwest corridor.

## Key Plan Policy Drivers

1. **Concentrate growth in existing developed areas** — Objective 1 of the Agricultural Resources chapter: "Protect and preserve agricultural land by locating new development in and around existing developed areas."

2. **Farmland Preservation Zoning Districts** lock down 5 towns (Brillion, Charlestown, Chilton, Rantoul, Woodville) — new development restricted to ag, open space, and utility uses.

3. **HALT Agricultural Enterprise Area** (28,217 acres) covers Towns of Brillion, Chilton, Rantoul, and Woodville — DATCP-designated, 10-year farmland preservation agreements with tax credits. This is a hard barrier to growth in the south/west.

4. **New mixed-use categories** in the FLU map expand flexibility on agricultural properties for low-impact businesses — but this is about rural economic vitality, not suburban subdivision.

5. **Housing goal:** diversify beyond 83.2% single-family — plan calls for duplexes, quadplexes, and small-scale multi-unit in urban areas to address 19% cost-burdened households.

6. **Infrastructure follows growth:** CTH KK and CTH AP arterial improvements are planned in the Harrison corridor; sewer service areas are concentrated in the northwest (Appleton, Heart of the Valley, Neenah-Menasha SSAs).

## Bottom Line

- **Appleton's southward expansion is the clear and dominant growth driver** — not a question, it's the documented reality. The Village of Harrison is the epicenter.
- **Lake Winnebago shoreline is a different, smaller-scale dynamic** — seasonal/second-home amenity development, not large-lot subdivision. Stockbridge and Brothertown have their own plans outside the county comp plan.
- **The direct growth path follows US 10/US 41 south from Appleton through Harrison and into the northwest corner of the county.** The southern and eastern agricultural towns are under active farmland preservation — they are NOT in the growth path by design.
- **The 2045 plan is still in draft** (Land Use chapter marked "Coming Soon!" as of mid-2025), but policy direction is clear: funnel growth north, preserve the rest.

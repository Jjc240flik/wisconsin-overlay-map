# Rock County Future Growth Direction — FLU & Comp Plan Analysis
**Date:** July 12, 2026 | **Sources:** County Comp Plan, Farmland Preservation Plan (2026 draft), Janesville Comp Plan (2023), Beloit Comp Plan 2045, Town FLU maps

---

## 1. Is the Stateline Corridor (I-39/90) the Primary Growth Driver?

**YES — unequivocally.** The I-39/90 corridor is the dominant growth axis in Rock County, confirmed by every planning document reviewed:

- **Rock County Comp Plan 2035** identifies I-39/90 as the primary corridor bisecting the county north-south through Janesville and Beloit
- **Janesville 2023 Comp Plan** explicitly directs **industrial expansion "primarily to the south of the City"** (toward the I-39/90 corridor and Beloit), and designates its southeast edge at I-39/90
- **Farmland Preservation Plan (Preserve Rock 2025-2035)** confirms proximity to Madison, Milwaukee, Rockford, and Chicago via I-39/90 and I-43 means "Rock County will continue to grow" despite projected population decline
- **Beloit 2045 Comp Plan** (under update, Vandewalle & Associates) is focused on 20-year Stateline growth
- **Town of Beloit** is one of only **three** entities in the county operating a major sanitary sewer system (>10,000 residents), alongside Janesville and Beloit cities — infrastructure reserved for the growth corridor

**Bottom line:** Growth flows north-south along I-39/90 between the Illinois line → Beloit → Janesville, with a secondary east-west axis along US-14. The Stateline corridor is the **primary engine** of land conversion, residential demand, and industrial/logistics expansion.

## 3. FLU Analysis by Town

### Town of Beloit — HIGHEST GROWTH PRESSURE
- **FLU designations found in planning documents:** Urban Mixed-Use, Urban Moderate Density, Campus
- **Infrastructure:** One of only 3 major sewer systems in the county (>10,000 residents)
- **Energy:** Alliant Energy 300+ acre campus + Paddock Solar Project
- **Location:** Directly adjacent to City of Beloit and Illinois state line. I-39/90 and I-43 intersection.
- **Rezoning pipeline:** 116 parcels (A+ rated). Top parcels: 117ac, 105ac, 80ac, 80ac, 80ac — all adjacent to Beloit city limits, marked for Ag→Residential conversion.
- **Key finding:** The Town of Beloit is effectively designated as an urban expansion area. The FLU map shows urban-density categories, not agricultural preservation.

### Town of Janesville — HIGH GROWTH PRESSURE
- **FLU:** Residential expansion surrounding the city. Planned Neighborhoods on South, West, Central, and North sides.
- **Janesville Comp Plan** projects 6,318 new residents (+3,836 households) by 2040. Needs 1,648 residential acres + 2,622 non-residential acres (2020-2040). 10,000+ acres total through 2060.
- **Growth direction:** Historically north and east, but Plan seeks to **rebalance toward south and west** (toward I-39/90). Industrial expansion primarily south.
- **Urban Reserve Areas** designated beyond 40-year growth boundary.
- **Logical edges:** I-39/90 (SE), planned STH 11-US 14 connector (W), Airport (S), City of Milton (N).
- **Rezoning pipeline:** 151 parcels (A rated). Top parcels: 157ac, 132ac, 110ac, 95ac, 91ac.

### Town of Turtle — HIGH GROWTH PRESSURE
- **FLU:** Southern portion near Beloit designated for growth. Part of LaPrairie Agricultural Enterprise Area (over 21,000 acres).
- **Location:** Between Beloit and Janesville along I-39/90. Southern edge directly adjacent to Beloit city.
- **Rezoning pipeline:** 190 parcels (A rated). Top parcels: 161ac, 100ac, 90ac, 89ac, 88ac.
- **Key finding:** Turtle is split — northern portion is agriculturally preserved (AEA), southern portion is in the direct Stateline growth path.

### Town of Harmony — MODERATE-HIGH
- **FLU:** Edge growth east of Janesville. Mix of agricultural preservation and residential expansion.
- **Energy:** WE Energies Dawn Harvest solar/battery installation (operational late 2026/early 2027).
- **Rezoning pipeline:** 119 parcels (B+ rated).

### Village of Evansville — MODERATE-HIGH
- **FLU:** Growing bedroom community. Northwest metro expansion.
- **Economy:** Soybean capital of Wisconsin. Planned soybean crushing plant (on hold).
- **Rezoning pipeline:** 35 parcels (B+ rated).

### Town of Milton — MODERATE-HIGH
- **FLU:** Edge growth adjacent to City of Milton. North of Janesville.
- **Economy:** United Ethanol plant, Cargill livestock feed.
- **Rezoning pipeline:** 204 parcels (B+ rated) — largest cluster by count.

### Town of Fulton — MODERATE-HIGH
- **FLU:** Edge growth northeast of Janesville.
- **Energy:** North Rock County Solar Project (Alliant Energy).
- **Rezoning pipeline:** 130 parcels (B+ rated).

## 4. Key Cross-Cutting Findings

- **Population paradox:** DOA projects Rock County declining 11% by 2050 (to 145,770), but smaller households are driving demand for new housing units. The county's geographic position between Madison, Milwaukee, Rockford, and Chicago means growth pressure will continue regardless.
- **Sewer infrastructure dictates growth:** Only Janesville, Beloit, and Town of Beloit have major sewer systems. Where sewer goes, growth follows.
- **Farmland loss accelerating:** County land in farms dropped from 74.9% (2007) to 64.5% (2022) — a loss of 47,725 acres in 15 years. The Comp Plan explicitly acknowledges agricultural land conversion as the primary mechanism for urban expansion.
- **Agricultural preservation tension:** The Rock Prairie (La Prairie, Harmony, Johnstown, Bradford townships) has Plano Silt Loam — highly productive soil. The LaPrairie AEA (21,000+ acres) and Turtle AEA parcels are designated for preservation. This creates a clear east-west dividing line: growth to the south/west of Janesville, preservation to the east/southeast.
- **Renewable energy overlay:** Major solar projects in Beloit Town, Fulton, Harmony, and La Prairie add a competing land use pressure on agricultural land, further complicating the ag-preservation vs. growth equation.

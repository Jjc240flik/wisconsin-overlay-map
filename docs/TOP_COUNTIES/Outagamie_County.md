# Outagamie County – Future Property Development Vision

## General Breakdown: Outagamie County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Outagamie County's Comprehensive Plan 2040 ("The Shared Path"), municipal comprehensive plans, and the North Star / Cody Bjugan framework, here's a clear summary of what Outagamie County planners want to see in future development:

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Outagamie County wants to avoid sprawling, low-density development. New growth should be focused in areas that already have (or are planned to have) public sewer and water service. Infill and redevelopment are preferred over constant farmland conversion.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
A major goal is to preserve productive farmland where possible. Future Land Use plans show many areas remaining as Agricultural long-term. However, village and town edge-of-city locations are where farmland transitions to development.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Most future residential and commercial growth is planned around:
- **City of Appleton** (outward into Greenville, Grand Chute, Harrison, Freedom)
- **City of Kaukauna** (outward into Buchanan, Combined Locks, Little Chute)
- Villages directly adjacent to city limits (Greenville, Grand Chute) are the **primary growth zones**
- Smaller cities (New London, Seymour) have localized growth

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Planners want to see more mixed-use developments (residential + commercial together). Higher density is encouraged along US 41 corridor and near transit-served areas.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Future development is expected along key transportation routes such as:
- U.S. Highway 41 (primary growth corridor through Appleton > Greenville > Grand Chute)
- State Highway 96
- State Highway 47
- State Highway 10 (Harrison corridor)

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Outagamie County does not control zoning in incorporated areas. All actual zoning decisions are made by the individual cities, villages, and towns. The County's role is coordination and planning guidance for unincorporated areas.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

 | Priority | What Planners Want |
|---|---|
 | Infrastructure Efficiency | Develop where sewer, water, and roads already exist |
 | Agricultural Preservation | Protect good farmland from unnecessary development |
 | Housing Variety | Allow different housing types (single-family, multi-family, mixed-use) |
 | Economic Growth | Support commercial and industrial development in planned areas |
 | Environmental Protection | Avoid development in wetlands, floodplains, and sensitive areas |

**Bottom Line**
Outagamie County's growth corridor follows US 41 and radiates outward from Appleton and Kaukauna. The highest subdivision potential is in villages and towns directly adjacent to city limits — Greenville, Grand Chute, Harrison, Freedom, Buchanan, Combined Locks, and Little Chute.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas (Priority 1 — Direct City Edge):**
Greenville (Village), Grand Chute (Town), Harrison (Village), Combined Locks (Village), Little Chute (Village) — These are the villages and towns directly outside Appleton and Kaukauna city limits where future land use maps show the strongest development pressure. These are textbook Cody Bjugan targets.

**Moderate to High Growth Areas (Priority 2 — Adjacent to Growth):**
Buchanan (Town), Freedom (Town), Kimberly (Village) — Directly adjacent to city limits or metro core. Should be researched for specific FLU and sewer boundaries.

**Moderate Growth Areas:**
Vandenbroek, Hortonville, Wrightstown, Center — Secondary growth rings. May have localized development nodes.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:**
Black Creek, Oneida, Bear Creek, Nichols, Shiocton — Rural villages and towns far from Appleton/Kaukauna metro. Agricultural preservation likely dominant.

**Built-Out Communities:**
City of Appleton and City of Kaukauna are largely developed. Future growth there will be redevelopment and infill — the real subdivision opportunity is in the surrounding villages and towns.

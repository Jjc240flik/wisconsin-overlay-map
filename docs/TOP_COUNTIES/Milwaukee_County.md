# Milwaukee County – Future Property Development Vision
## TOP COUNTY – Priority Land Research Target

## General Breakdown: Milwaukee County's Vision for Future Property Development

Milwaukee County is Wisconsin's most urbanized county. Growth is primarily redevelopment, infill, and densification rather than greenfield expansion. The county has limited undeveloped land, but major redevelopment zones exist.

### Key Development Zones
- **Harbor District** (Milwaukee lakefront redevelopment)
- **Menomonee Valley** (industrial to mixed-use)
- **30th Street Corridor** (industrial redevelopment)
- **Downtown Milwaukee** (high-rise residential/commercial)
- **County line edges** (limited edge growth at north and west boundaries)

### Key Priorities
| Priority | What Planners Want |
|---|---|
| Infill & Redevelopment | Primary growth strategy |
| Transit-Oriented | Along BRT and streetcar corridors |
| Affordable Housing | Major policy focus |
| Environmental Remediation | Brownfield redevelopment |
| Mixed-Use | Commercial + residential integration |

**Bottom Line**
Milwaukee County has limited greenfield subdivision potential. The opportunity is in redevelopment parcels, brownfield sites, and edge-growth areas at the county line where it meets Waukesha, Ozaukee, and Washington counties. Most true subdivision targets will be in surrounding counties, not Milwaukee County itself.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Most Growth Potential in Milwaukee County:**
Franklin (City), Oak Creek (City) — These are the only municipalities with significant undeveloped land.

**Moderate Growth (Redevelopment):**
City of Milwaukee (Harbor District, Menomonee Valley, 30th Street Corridor).

**Low Growth:**
Most suburbs are fully built out. Greenfield subdivision targets are best found in **Waukesha, Ozaukee, and Washington counties**, not Milwaukee County.

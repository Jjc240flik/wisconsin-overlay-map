# Calumet County – Future Property Development Vision
## TOP COUNTY – Priority Land Research Target

## General Breakdown: Calumet County's Vision for Future Property Development

Calumet County is in the Fox Valley between Lake Winnebago and the Appleton metro. Growth is driven by its position between two major economic centers (Appleton and Oshkosh/Fond du Lac). The county has limited land area but strategic location.

### Key Growth Corridors
- US 41/44 corridor (Appleton → Chilton → Fond du Lac)
- Lakeshore communities along Lake Winnebago (seasonal and year-round)
- Highway 55 corridor

### Key Priorities
 | Priority | What Planners Want |
|---|---|
 | Infrastructure Efficiency | Focus growth in areas with utilities |
 | Lake Winnebago Access | Manage lakeshore development |
 | Agricultural Preservation | Protect farmland |
 | Housing Variety | Support Fox Valley commuters |

**Bottom Line**
Highest subdivision potential is in towns adjacent to Lake Winnebago and the Brillion/Chilton corridor, plus the towns on the northern edge near Appleton. Western shoreline communities have seasonal growth.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas (Priority 1):**
Harrison (Town), Menasha (Calumet Town) — Directly adjacent to Appleton/Fox Cities metro on the northern edge.

**Moderate to High:**
Stockbridge, Brothertown — Lake Winnebago shoreline communities with growth potential.

**Moderate:**
Chilton, Brillion, Hilbert, Sherwood — Localized growth centers.

**Low Growth:**
Southwestern and agricultural towns (New Holstein, Charlestown, Rantoul, Woodville).

## Growth Direction & FLU Analysis

*Research based on county 2040 comprehensive plans, FLU maps, and municipal planning documents. Last updated: 2026-07-13.*

### Growth Direction
**Appleton's southward expansion IS the dominant growth driver.** The Village of Harrison alone accounts for >50% of county population growth since 2000 (doubled to 12,500). 79.5% of workers commute to the Fox Cities. The Lake Winnebago shoreline (Stockbridge, Brothertown) is seasonal/second-home amenity — NOT large-scale subdivision.

### Growth Corridors

| Corridor | Towns | Intensity & Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| US 41/10 NW | Harrison Town, Menasha Town | PRIMARY — South of Appleton. US 41/10 corridor. Strong ag→residential rezoning potential. |
| Lake Winnebago | Stockbridge, Brothertown | DOWNGRADED — Seasonal/second-home amenity, NOT subdivision. Environmental constraints (Niagara Escarpment, quarries, wetlands). |

### Towns NOT in the Growth Path

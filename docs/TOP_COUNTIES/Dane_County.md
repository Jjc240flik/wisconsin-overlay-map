# Dane County – Future Property Development Vision
## TOP COUNTY – Priority Land Research Target

## General Breakdown: Dane County's Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Dane County's comprehensive planning documents and the North Star / Cody Bjugan framework, here's a clear summary of what Dane County planners want to see in future development:

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Dane County wants to avoid sprawling, low-density development around Madison. New growth should be focused in areas that already have (or are planned to have) public sewer and water service. The county prefers infill and redevelopment over constantly expanding into farmland.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
A major goal is to preserve productive farmland, particularly in the Town of Dunn, Town of Primrose, and areas away from the Madison metro. Future Land Use plans show many areas remaining as Agricultural long-term.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Most future residential and commercial growth is planned around:
- **City of Madison** (outward into villages and towns on all sides)
- **City of Middleton** (outward into Westport and Springfield)
- **City of Sun Prairie** (outward into Bristol and Sun Prairie town)
- **Villages directly adjacent to city limits** (Fitchburg, Middleton, Verona, Sun Prairie, McFarland, Waunakee, Oregon, Stoughton)

### 4. Support Transit-Oriented Development
Dane County (especially Madison) strongly supports higher-density, mixed-use development along bus rapid transit (BRT) corridors and near transit hubs.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Future development is expected along key transportation routes:
- I-39/90/94 (north-south through the county)
- US 12/18 (beltline around Madison)
- US 14, US 151, Highway 19, Highway A

### 6. Key Priorities for Future Development
| Priority | What Planners Want |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Focus growth in areas with existing sewer service |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect the agricultural economy and farmland |
| Housing Variety | Allow diverse housing types to address demand |
| Transit-Oriented | Support BRT and non-auto commuting |
| Environmental Protection | Avoid wetlands, floodplains, and sensitive habitats |

**Bottom Line**
Dane County's massive and sustained growth is concentrated in the ring of villages directly around Madison. The highest subdivision potential is in villages and towns on the metro edge — Fitchburg, Verona, Middleton, Sun Prairie, Waunakee, DeForest, McFarland, Oregon, Stoughton, and Cottage Grove.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas (Priority 1 — Direct City Edge):**
Fitchburg, Verona, Sun Prairie (Town), Westport, Springfield, Burke, Blooming Grove — These are the towns and villages directly outside Madison city limits where the strongest farmland-to-residential transition is happening.

**Moderate to High Growth Areas (Priority 2 — Adjacent to Growth):**
Waunakee, DeForest, Windsor, Oregon, McFarland, Pleasant Springs, Dunn, Montrose, Bristol, Cottage Grove, Cross Plains — Edge-adjacent communities near the metro ring.

**Moderate Growth Areas:**
Stoughton, Dane, Black Earth, Mazomanie — Secondary growth rings or localized growth.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:**
Towns far from the Madison metro (Primrose, Perry, Vermont, Albion, Berry, Christiana, Dunkirk, etc.) — agricultural preservation dominant.

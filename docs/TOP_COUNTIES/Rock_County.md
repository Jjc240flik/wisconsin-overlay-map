# Rock County – Future Property Development Vision
## TOP COUNTY – Priority Land Research Target

## General Breakdown: Rock County's Vision for Future Property Development

Rock County is in Wisconsin's south-central region, anchored by the Janesville-Beloit metro corridor along I-39/90. Growth is driven by Chicago-Madison commuters and the Stateline industrial corridor.

### Key Growth Corridors
- I-39/90 corridor (Janesville → Beloit → Illinois line)
- US 14 corridor
- Highway 51 corridor

### Key Priorities
 | Priority | What Planners Want |
|---|---|
 | Stateline Growth | Capitalize on Illinois-to-Wisconsin migration |
 | Industrial Expansion | Support manufacturing and logistics |
 | Agricultural Preservation | Protect farmland in western townships |
 | Housing Variety | Mix of housing for growing population |

**Bottom Line**
Highest subdivision potential is in towns directly adjacent to Janesville and Beloit, and along the I-39/90/Stateline corridor. Northern and western towns are more agricultural with limited development pressure.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas (Priority 1):**
Town of Beloit, Town of Janesville, Town of Turtle — Directly adjacent to city limits along the Stateline/I-39/90 corridor.

**Moderate to High:**
Harmony, Evansville, Milton (Town), Fulton — Edge-adjacent growth centers.

**Low Growth:**
Western and northern towns (Avon, Bradford, Center, Johnstown, Lima, Magnolia, Newark, Plymouth, Porter) — Agricultural and rural.

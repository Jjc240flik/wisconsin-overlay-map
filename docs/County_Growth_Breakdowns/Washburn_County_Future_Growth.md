# Washburn County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs forest comprehensive land use plan / zoning map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Washburn County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Washburn County planning resources, the county does not maintain a Future Land Use GIS layer. The county forest comprehensive land use plan and zoning department provide the primary regulatory framework.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Without a centralized future land use layer, planning should be guided by county general zoning, the forest comp plan, and municipal-level comprehensive plans.

### 2. Protect Forest / Lake / Agricultural Land
Washburn County Forest Comprehensive Land Use Plan 2021–2035 governs management activities on county forest lands.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Shell Lake
- Spooner
- Minong
- Birchwood
- Highway 53 / 63 / 70 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is likely near Shell Lake, Spooner, and highway corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 53
- State Highway 63
- State Highway 70

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The county does not maintain a countywide FLU layer. Municipal comprehensive plans and zoning maps must be checked for parcel-level conclusions.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near communities and corridor nodes |
| Forest / Lake Preservation | Forest comp plan and shoreland/zoning apply |
| Housing Variety | Verify near community nodes via town/city plans |
| Economic Growth | Tourism, corridor, and community-serving nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Lakes, forests, shoreland, wetlands |

**Bottom Line**  
Washburn County requires municipal-level plan review since there is no countywide future land use GIS layer. Best opportunities are near Shell Lake, Spooner, and highway corridor nodes.

# Vernon County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / farmland preservation plan map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Vernon County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the Vernon County Comprehensive Plan (adopted February 2025) and related resources, the county plan recommends adoption of several land use strategies, a Farmland Preservation Ordinance, and a Prohibition of 30% Slope Disturbance.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth must account for the county’s highly sloped, driftless-area landscape. Future development should be guided by the comprehensive plan, slope prohibitions, and farmland preservation strategies.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land / Steep Slope Environment
Vernon County is uniquely challenging: steep slopes (30%+ prohibition) and farmland preservation are major constraints.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Viroqua
- Westby
- Cashton
- Stoddard / De Soto
- Coon Valley
- Highway 14 / 27 / 35 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity development is most likely near Viroqua, Westby, and the limited developable corridor areas.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 14
- State Highway 27
- Highway 35 (Mississippi River)
- Highway 162

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The county comprehensive plan provides the framework; farmland preservation, slope, and municipal zoning all apply.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Viroqua, Westby, and corridor nodes |
| Agricultural / Slope Preservation | Steep slope disturbance prohibition; farmland preservation ordinance |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned expansion in limited developable areas |
| Economic Growth | Corridor and community-serving nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Driftless-area bluffs, river corridors, Mississippi River, wetlands |

**Bottom Line**  
Vernon County is a low-growth, high-constraint county due to steep slopes and farmland preservation. Best targets are near Viroqua, Westby, and limited corridor nodes.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Viroqua, Westby, selected corridor nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Cashton, limited developable corridor areas.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Driftless agricultural/bluff towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Viroqua and Westby should be checked for infill.

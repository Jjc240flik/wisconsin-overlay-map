# St. Croix County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / generalized future land use map extraction.

## General Breakdown: St. Croix County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the St. Croix County Comprehensive Plan 2024–2045 and related resources, the county plan establishes long-term policies for land use through the land use chapter and Generalized Future Local Land Use map.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should align with the comprehensive plan, generalized future land use map, and zoning code implementation.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
St. Croix County has significant rural and agricultural areas, especially in its eastern and northern towns.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Hudson
- River Falls (Pierce County edge)
- New Richmond
- Baldwin
- Somerset
- I-94 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-density and mixed-use growth is most likely near Hudson, New Richmond, and I-94 interchange communities.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-94
- Highway 35
- Highway 12
- Highway 63

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The county zoning code implements comprehensive plan policies. Municipal comprehensive plans and zoning apply to incorporated areas.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near I-94 corridor and metro-edge communities |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect rural/agricultural land outside growth corridors |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential expansion near corridor nodes |
| Economic Growth | I-94 business parks and corridor commercial growth |
| Environmental Protection | St. Croix River, Lake St. Croix, shoreland, wetlands, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
St. Croix County is one of the highest-growth counties in Wisconsin due to Twin Cities exurban expansion. Best targets are near Hudson, New Richmond, Somerset, and I-94 corridor communities.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Hudson, New Richmond, Somerset, I-94 corridor.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Baldwin, selected corridor villages.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns away from corridors.

**Built-Out Communities:** Hudson should be checked for infill plus edge growth.

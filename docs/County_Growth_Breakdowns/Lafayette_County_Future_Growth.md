# Lafayette County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan and draft zoning map review.

## General Breakdown: Lafayette County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Lafayette County comprehensive plan and land use/zoning code resources, future development research should focus on consistency between the comprehensive plan, land use powers, zoning code updates, and future zoning maps.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Development should be checked against adopted comprehensive plans and the county’s zoning / land use code update process.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Lafayette County is strongly agricultural; farmland preservation versus future development transition is the primary screening issue.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Darlington
- Shullsburg
- Belmont
- Argyle
- South Wayne
- Highway 11 / 23 / 81 corridors

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Any higher-intensity development should be verified near existing cities/villages and mapped future development areas.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 11
- Highway 23
- Highway 81
- Highway 151 influence near Belmont / Platteville edge

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Comprehensive plan consistency is central. County and municipal zoning/future maps must both be checked before parcel targeting.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near communities and mapped service/corridor areas |
| Agricultural Preservation | Avoid unnecessary farmland fragmentation |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential growth areas |
| Economic Growth | Identify commercial/industrial nodes near highways |
| Environmental Protection | Avoid floodplain, wetlands, steep slopes, and sensitive rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Lafayette County should be researched as an agricultural county where future development value depends heavily on official comp plan consistency and draft/current zoning map direction.

**Highest Growth Areas:** Darlington, Shullsburg, Belmont/corridor areas.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Darlington and Shullsburg should be checked for infill and edge expansion.

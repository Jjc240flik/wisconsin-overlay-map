# Pepin County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / future land use map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Pepin County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Pepin County comprehensive planning documents and implementation materials, the county completed their comprehensive plan in 2013 with planning support from the Mississippi River Regional Planning Commission.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Future land use decisions should be based on and be consistent with the current land use plan and comprehensive plan guidance.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Pepin County has significant agricultural and rural river valley land use considerations.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Durand
- Pepin
- Stockholm
- Highway 10 / 35 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is most likely near Durand, Pepin, and highway/river corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 10
- Highway 35 (Great River Road)
- Highway 183

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County comprehensive plan provides the framework; municipal zoning and local plans must be checked for parcel-level decisions.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Durand, Pepin, and Highway 10 corridor |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect Mississippi River valley farmland and rural areas |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential expansion areas |
| Economic Growth | River tourism/corridor and agricultural service nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Mississippi River, shoreland, wetlands, floodplain, and blufflands |

**Bottom Line**  
Pepin County should be researched as a Mississippi River valley agricultural/rural county with growth potential near Durand, Pepin, and Highway 10 corridor nodes.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Durand, Pepin, and Highway 10 corridor.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Stockholm and selected river corridor nodes.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Mississippi River valley agricultural towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Durand and Pepin should be checked for infill and edge growth.

# Vilas County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / future land use map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Vilas County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the Vilas County Comprehensive Plan and zoning/planning resources, the plan includes updated maps including existing and future land use.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be guided by the comprehensive plan and existing/future land use maps. Unincorporated areas are under county zoning.

### 2. Protect Lake / Forest / Rural Land
Vilas County is one of Wisconsin's most lake-dense counties. Lake protection, shoreland regulation, and forest/rural preservation are central.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Eagle River
- Lac du Flambeau
- Phelps
- Boulder Junction
- Highway 45 / 51 / 70 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity development is most likely near Eagle River and corridor service nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 45
- US Highway 51
- State Highway 70

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County zoning & planning enforces regulations in unincorporated areas. Local municipal plans also apply where incorporated.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near communities and corridor nodes |
| Lake / Forest Preservation | Protect lakes, shoreland, forest, and rural resources |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned near community service nodes |
| Economic Growth | Tourism, recreation, and community-serving commercial |
| Environmental Protection | Lakes, shoreland, forests, wetlands, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Vilas County should be researched as a lake/forest/tourism county with strongest targets near Eagle River and highway corridor nodes.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Eagle River, highway corridor nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Lac du Flambeau, select lake/resort communities.

**Low Growth / Lake Preservation Areas:** Forest/lake/rural resource areas.

**Built-Out Communities:** Eagle River should be checked for infill and edge growth.

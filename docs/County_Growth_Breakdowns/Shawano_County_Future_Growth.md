# Shawano County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / future land use map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Shawano County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Shawano County comprehensive plan and related town/city resources, the county plan includes existing and future land use maps, farmland preservation direction, and land use categories to guide future zoning decisions.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be guided by the county comprehensive plan, future land use maps, and municipal-level planning.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Shawano County comprehensive & farmland preservation materials indicate agricultural preservation is a central issue.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Shawano
- Clintonville
- Bonduel
- Marion
- Highway 29 / 22 / 55 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-density uses are most likely near Shawano, Clintonville, and Highway 29 corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 29
- Highway 22
- Highway 55

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County comprehensive plan and town-level comprehensive plans (e.g., Town of Red Springs) both apply for future land use.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Shawano, Clintonville, and Highway 29 corridor |
| Agricultural Preservation | Use county comprehensive & farmland preservation maps |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential areas in comp plans |
| Economic Growth | Corridor commercial/industrial nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Wolf River, lakes, wetlands, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Shawano County should be researched as a Shawano/Clintonville + Highway 29 corridor county, with strong farmland preservation context.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Shawano, Clintonville, Highway 29 corridor.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Bonduel, Marion, selected corridor towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural areas.

**Built-Out Communities:** Shawano and Clintonville should be checked for infill and edge growth.

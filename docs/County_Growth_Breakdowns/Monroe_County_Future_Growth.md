# Monroe County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs Future Land Use Map and farmland preservation plan extraction.

## General Breakdown: Monroe County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Monroe County zoning resources, the county maintains a Future Land Use Map and Farmland Preservation Plan appendix tied to comprehensive planning.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be evaluated against the Future Land Use Map and county zoning/farmland preservation documents.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Monroe County’s Farmland Preservation Plan is a major source for identifying areas intended to remain agricultural.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Sparta
- Tomah
- Cashton
- Warrens
- I-90 / I-94 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is likely near Sparta, Tomah, and interstate-access areas.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-90
- I-94
- Highway 16
- Highway 21
- Highway 27

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The Future Land Use Map, farmland preservation plan, and municipal plans/zoning need to be compared for parcel-level conclusions.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Sparta, Tomah, and interstate nodes |
| Agricultural Preservation | Use Farmland Preservation Plan and Future Land Use Map |
| Housing Variety | Verify residential expansion near cities/villages |
| Economic Growth | Interstate logistics/commercial/industrial nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Wetlands, floodplain, steep slopes, and rural landscape constraints |

**Bottom Line**  
Monroe County is a strong county for the current-vs-future comparison because official zoning resources reference both a Future Land Use Map and a Farmland Preservation Plan.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Tomah, Sparta, and I-90/I-94 corridor nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Cashton, Warrens, and selected corridor towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns away from services/corridors.

**Built-Out Communities:** Sparta and Tomah should be checked for infill/redevelopment and mapped edge growth.

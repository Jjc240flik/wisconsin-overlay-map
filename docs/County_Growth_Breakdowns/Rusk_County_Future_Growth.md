# Rusk County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan update / kickoff packet extraction.

## General Breakdown: Rusk County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Rusk County comprehensive plan update materials, the county is in the process of updating its comprehensive plan as required by state law (minimum every 10 years).

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be reevaluated as part of the comprehensive plan update process, with attention to current versus future land use mapping.

### 2. Protect Agricultural / Forest Land
Rusk County has significant agricultural, forest, and rural resource lands. Preservation is likely central to the comprehensive plan direction.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Ladysmith
- Bruce
- Hawkins
- Weyerhaeuser
- Highway 8 / 27 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Any intensive development is likely near Ladysmith and highway corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 8
- State Highway 27
- State Highway 40

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The county plan update is a key source, but municipal zoning and town-level plans should also be checked.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Ladysmith and corridor nodes |
| Agricultural / Forest Preservation | Protect rural and resource lands |
| Housing Variety | Verify near communities in plan update |
| Economic Growth | Corridor and community-serving nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Forest, agricultural, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Rusk County is a low-growth, rural/forest county. Best opportunities are near Ladysmith and Highway 8 corridor nodes, with planning update review essential.

# Trempealeau County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / town-level land use map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Trempealeau County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Trempealeau County planning resources, the county comprehensive plan includes future land use maps prepared for each town. The county land use map was compiled from 15 individual town maps.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be evaluated through town-level future land use maps and the county comprehensive plan.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Trempealeau County has significant agricultural areas, especially in its rural interior and Mississippi River valley areas.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Arcadia
- Whitehall
- Blair
- Trempealeau (village)
- Independence
- Osseo
- Highway 53 / 93 / 95 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity development is most likely near Arcadia, Whitehall, and highway corridor service nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 53
- State Highway 93
- State Highway 95
- I-94 / Osseo area

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Town-level comprehensive plans and future land use maps are the primary decision-making framework.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Arcadia, Whitehall, and corridor nodes |
| Agricultural Preservation | Town-level FLU maps identify growth vs preservation |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential areas in town maps |
| Economic Growth | Corridor and community-serving nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Mississippi River, shoreland, bluffs, wetlands, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Trempealeau County should be researched through its 15 town-level future land use maps. Best targets are near Arcadia, Whitehall, and highway corridor service nodes.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Arcadia, Trempealeau, Whitehall, Osseo.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Blair, Independence, selected corridor towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural/bluff towns away from corridors.

**Built-Out Communities:** Arcadia and Trempealeau should be checked for infill and edge growth.

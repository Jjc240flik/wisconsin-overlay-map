# Ozaukee County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs multi-jurisdictional comprehensive plan map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Ozaukee County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Ozaukee County’s Final Comprehensive Plan 2035 and multi-jurisdictional comprehensive plan resources, the county integrates municipal planned land use maps into a single Land Use Plan Map for 2035.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should align with the 2035 comprehensive plan, planned land use maps, and infrastructure capacity near Lake Michigan corridor communities.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Ozaukee County has significant rural/agricultural areas in its western towns while the eastern lake corridor is more developed.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Port Washington
- Cedarburg
- Grafton
- Mequon
- Saukville
- I-43 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-density and mixed-use growth is likely near the Lake Michigan corridor, I-43 interchange areas, and existing downtown/service nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-43
- Highway 60
- Highway 57
- Highway 32 / Lake Drive corridor

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The comprehensive plan integrates municipal planned land use maps, but city/village/town zoning must be checked for parcel-level conclusions.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near I-43 corridor and lakefront communities |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect rural/agricultural land in western towns |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential expansion near corridor communities |
| Economic Growth | I-43 business/industrial parks and corridor nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Lake Michigan shoreland, river corridors, wetlands, and rural resources |

**Bottom Line**  
Ozaukee County should be researched as a Lake Michigan I-43 corridor county with strongest targets near Port Washington, Cedarburg, Grafton, Mequon, and Saukville.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Grafton, Port Washington, Cedarburg, Mequon, Saukville.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** I-43 interchange and service nodes.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Western rural towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Port Washington, Cedarburg, and Mequon should be checked for infill plus edge growth.

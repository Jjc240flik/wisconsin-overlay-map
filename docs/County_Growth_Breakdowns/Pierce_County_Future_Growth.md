# Pierce County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / chapter 232 extraction.

## General Breakdown: Pierce County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Pierce County comprehensive plan and land management resources, the county has adopted an updated comprehensive plan through Chapter 232 of county code.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be guided by the comprehensive plan and applied to land use decisions county-wide, focusing on corridors and service areas.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Pierce County has strong agricultural and rural resource considerations. Farmland preservation and future land use mapping are both central.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- River Falls
- Ellsworth
- Prescott
- Spring Valley
- Highway 10 / 29 / 35 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is likely near River Falls, Prescott, and highway corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 10
- State Highway 29
- Highway 35 (Great River Road)
- Highway 63

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County comprehensive plan is the framework, but municipal/city/village/town zoning decisions also apply.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near River Falls, Prescott, Ellsworth, and highway corridors |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect agricultural and rural resource lands |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential expansion areas |
| Economic Growth | River Falls university/corridor growth and business nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Mississippi River, shoreland, wetlands, floodplain, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Pierce County should be researched as a Twin Cities-adjacent corridor county with strong growth potential near River Falls, Prescott, and US Highway 10.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** River Falls, Prescott, Ellsworth, Highway 10 corridor.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Spring Valley, selected corridor towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural Mississippi River valley and agricultural towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** River Falls and Prescott should be checked for infill plus edge growth.

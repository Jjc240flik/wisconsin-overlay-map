# Waukesha County – Future Property Development Vision

## General Breakdown: Waukesha County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Waukesha County’s comprehensive planning documents and future land use information, here’s a clear summary of what Waukesha County planners want to see in future development:

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Waukesha County wants to avoid sprawling, low-density development across rural areas. New growth should be focused in areas that already have (or are planned to have) public sewer and water service. They prefer infill and redevelopment over constantly expanding into farmland.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
A major goal is to preserve productive farmland where possible. Future Land Use plans show many areas remaining as Agricultural long-term. Growth should be directed to areas that are less suitable for farming.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Most future residential and commercial growth is planned around:
- City of Waukesha
- Cities and villages in the eastern and central parts of the county
- Areas with access to major transportation corridors

These areas are expected to see the majority of new housing and business development.

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Planners want to see more mixed-use developments (residential + commercial together). They encourage higher density in appropriate locations to make better use of infrastructure.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Future development is expected along key transportation routes such as:
- I-94
- Highway 16
- Highway 18
- Highway 83

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Important point: Waukesha County does not control zoning in incorporated areas. All actual zoning decisions are made by the individual cities, villages, and towns. The County’s role is more about coordination and planning guidance for unincorporated areas.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority                    | What Planners Want                                      |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Infrastructure Efficiency  | Develop where sewer, water, and roads already exist     |
| Agricultural Preservation  | Protect good farmland from unnecessary development      |
| Housing Variety            | Allow different housing types (single-family, multi-family, mixed-use) |
| Economic Growth            | Support commercial and industrial development in planned areas |
| Environmental Protection   | Avoid development in wetlands, floodplains, and sensitive areas |

**Bottom Line**  
Waukesha County planners want controlled, efficient growth that protects farmland, uses existing infrastructure, happens mostly around existing population centers, and is coordinated between the County and local municipalities.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:**  
Menomonee Falls, Sussex, and areas near the City of Waukesha — These are the main areas where new residential and commercial development is expected.

**Moderate Growth Areas:**  
Brookfield, Delafield, and Mukwonago areas.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:**  
Lisbon, Merton, Oconomowoc, Genesee, and other western and northern towns.

**Built-Out Communities:**  
City of Waukesha and City of Brookfield are largely developed, so future growth will mostly be redevelopment and infill.

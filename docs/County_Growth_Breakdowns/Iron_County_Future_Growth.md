# Iron County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs official municipal map review before acquisition use.

## General Breakdown: Iron County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on available land-use planning leads, Iron County future development research should prioritize land use compatibility, rural preservation, lake/forest protection, and growth near existing communities and service nodes.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Iron County growth is likely limited and should be evaluated around existing communities, highway corridors, and areas already served or planned for infrastructure.

### 2. Protect Agricultural / Forest / Rural Land
For Iron County, preservation is not only agricultural; forest, lake, shoreland, and rural character protection are major constraints.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Hurley
- Mercer
- Montreal
- Pence / town-level service nodes

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Any higher-density or mixed-use development is most likely near incorporated or service-center communities rather than rural forestland.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 51
- Highway 77
- Highway 2 / Hurley area connections

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Future land use and zoning consistency must be checked at the county and town level, especially because many northern Wisconsin communities rely on county zoning plus town comprehensive plans.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Focus development near communities and existing services |
| Rural / Forest Preservation | Avoid unnecessary conversion of forest, shoreland, and rural areas |
| Housing Variety | Check local comp plans for housing near service nodes |
| Economic Growth | Tourism, recreation, and limited commercial nodes likely matter most |
| Environmental Protection | Lakes, wetlands, shoreland, floodplain, and forest resources are critical |

**Bottom Line**  
Iron County should be treated as a low-growth / high-resource-protection county. The best future development opportunities are likely near Hurley, Mercer, and existing service corridors, not scattered rural conversion.

| Municipality | Type | Expected Future Growth | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hurley | City | Low to Moderate | Primary county service node; verify redevelopment and edge growth. |
| Mercer | Town | Low to Moderate | Tourism/recreation-oriented growth; verify land use maps. |
| Montreal | City | Low | Limited growth expected; verify local/county zoning. |
| Pence | Town | Low | Rural/forest context; verify comp plan references. |
| Rural lake/forest towns | Town | Low | Environmental and shoreland constraints likely dominate. |

---

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Hurley and Mercer area service nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Highway corridors with existing services.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Forested/rural interior and shoreland-sensitive areas.

**Built-Out Communities:** Hurley / Montreal should be reviewed for infill and redevelopment.

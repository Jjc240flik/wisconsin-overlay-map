# Brown County – Future Property Development Vision

## General Breakdown: Brown County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the 2040 Comprehensive Plan and the Future Land Use Maps (Appendix A), here’s a clear summary of what Brown County planners want to see in future development:

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Brown County wants to avoid sprawling, low-density development. New growth should be focused in areas that already have (or are planned to have) public sewer and water service. They prefer infill and redevelopment over constantly expanding into farmland.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
A major goal is to preserve productive farmland where possible. Future Land Use maps show many areas remaining as Agricultural long-term. Growth should be directed to areas that are less suitable for farming.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Most future residential and commercial growth is planned around:
- Green Bay
- De Pere
- Villages like Suamico, Howard, Allouez, and Bellevue

These areas are expected to see the majority of new housing and business development.

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Planners want to see more mixed-use developments (residential + commercial together). They encourage higher density in appropriate locations to make better use of infrastructure.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Future development is expected along key transportation routes such as:
- I-43
- Highway 41/29
- Highway GV
- Other major arterials

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Important point: Brown County does not control zoning. All actual zoning decisions are made by the individual cities, villages, and towns. The County’s role is more about coordination and planning guidance.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority                    | What Planners Want                                      |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Infrastructure Efficiency  | Develop where sewer, water, and roads already exist     |
| Agricultural Preservation  | Protect good farmland from unnecessary development      |
| Housing Variety            | Allow different housing types (single-family, multi-family, mixed-use) |
| Economic Growth            | Support commercial and industrial development in planned areas |
| Environmental Protection   | Avoid development in wetlands, floodplains, and sensitive areas |

**Bottom Line**  
Brown County planners want controlled, efficient growth that protects farmland, uses existing infrastructure, happens mostly around existing population centers, and is coordinated between the County and the 25 local municipalities.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:**  
Ledgeview, Lawrence, Suamico, Howard, and Scott — These are the main areas where new residential and commercial development is expected to occur outward from Green Bay and De Pere.

**Moderate Growth Areas:**  
Bellevue, Humboldt, and the Town of Green Bay.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:**  
Eaton, Glenmore, Holland, Morrison, New Denmark, Pittsfield, and Rockland.

**Built-Out Communities:**  
Allouez is largely developed, so future growth will mostly be redevelopment/infill.

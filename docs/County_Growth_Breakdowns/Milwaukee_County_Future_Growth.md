# Milwaukee County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Countywide zoning/future land use is primarily municipal. Needs City/Village plan-by-plan review.

## General Breakdown: Milwaukee County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Milwaukee County future development research is different from rural counties: most land use authority and future zoning direction is controlled by cities and villages, especially the City of Milwaukee and suburban municipalities.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Because Milwaukee County is largely urbanized, future growth is mostly redevelopment, infill, adaptive reuse, higher-density housing, and transit/corridor-oriented development.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Agricultural land is not the primary issue in Milwaukee County. The key issue is redevelopment potential, underutilized land, industrial/commercial reuse, and neighborhood-scale plan direction.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Primary growth/redevelopment nodes to verify:
- City of Milwaukee
- Wauwatosa
- West Allis
- Greenfield
- Oak Creek
- Franklin
- South Milwaukee / Cudahy / St. Francis corridor

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
This is likely one of the strongest counties for mixed-use, multifamily, infill, and redevelopment-oriented future land use research.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-94
- I-43
- I-41 / 894
- Highway 100
- Lakefront / harbor / airport industrial corridors
- Transit and redevelopment corridors in Milwaukee

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Milwaukee County is highly municipal. Current zoning and future land use must be researched by municipality and neighborhood plan, not countywide farmland conversion.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Reuse existing urban infrastructure |
| Agricultural Preservation | Not central; focus on redevelopment and brownfield/infill |
| Housing Variety | Multifamily, mixed-use, affordable/workforce housing, infill |
| Economic Growth | Industrial/commercial redevelopment, airport/port/corridor sites |
| Environmental Protection | Brownfields, floodplain, lakefront, river corridors, stormwater constraints |

**Bottom Line**  
Milwaukee County should be treated as an urban redevelopment county. The highest value research is not farmland-to-subdivision conversion but future land use / zoning changes around infill, multifamily, industrial reuse, and corridor redevelopment.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Milwaukee, Oak Creek, Wauwatosa, Franklin, West Allis.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Greenfield, South Milwaukee, Cudahy, St. Francis.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Not applicable in the same way as rural counties.

**Built-Out Communities:** Most of the county is built out; future opportunity is redevelopment/infill.

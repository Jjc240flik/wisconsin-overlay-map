# Portage County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan land use maps extraction.

## General Breakdown: Portage County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the Portage County Comprehensive Plan 2024–2044 and related town plans, the county plan provides a 20-year guide addressing land use, zoning, infrastructure, and development opportunities.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Future development should align with county and town comprehensive plans, land use maps, and zoning ordinances rather than scattered rural conversion.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Portage County has significant agricultural land and town-level comprehensive plans that identify farmland preservation areas.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Stevens Point
- Plover
- Whiting
- Amherst
- I-39/Highway 10 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity uses are most likely near Stevens Point, Plover, and I-39/Highway 10 corridor interchanges.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-39/US 51
- Highway 10
- Highway 54
- Highway 66

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County comprehensive plan and town comprehensive plans both apply. Town plans often include specific future land use maps.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Stevens Point, Plover, and I-39/Highway 10 nodes |
| Agricultural Preservation | Town plans identify farmland preservation vs growth areas |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential expansion in town/county maps |
| Economic Growth | Corridor and interchange commercial/industrial nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Wisconsin River, wetlands, floodplain, and rural resource areas |

**Bottom Line**  
Portage County should be researched as a Stevens Point/Plover metro + I-39 corridor county, with strongest targets near existing communities and highway interchanges.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Plover, Stevens Point metro edge, I-39/Highway 10 interchanges.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Whiting, Amherst, selected corridor towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Stevens Point should be checked for redevelopment/infill plus planned edge expansion.

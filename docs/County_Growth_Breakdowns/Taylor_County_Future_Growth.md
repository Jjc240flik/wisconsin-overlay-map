# Taylor County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / future land use map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Taylor County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the Taylor County Comprehensive Plan and related resources, the county plan includes a future land use map that serves as a general guide — not a zoning map.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
The future land use map is intended as a general guide for future development; it is not official mapping or zoning.

### 2. Protect Agricultural / Forest Land
Taylor County has strong agricultural and forest resource considerations. Zoning and FLU maps should be compared.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Medford
- Rib Lake
- Gilman
- Stetsonville
- Highway 13 / 64 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is most likely near Medford and highway corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- State Highway 13
- State Highway 64
- State Highway 73

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The FLU map is not zoning. Current zoning and future land use must be compared for each target area.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Medford and corridor nodes |
| Agricultural / Forest Preservation | Protect rural resource lands |
| Housing Variety | Verify near Medford and communities |
| Economic Growth | Corridor and community-serving nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Forest, agricultural, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Taylor County is a low-growth, rural/agricultural/forest county. Best opportunities are near Medford and Highway 13 corridor nodes.

# Marquette County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan update / future land use zoning map review.

## General Breakdown: Marquette County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Marquette County comprehensive plan update materials, the county is actively updating its 20-year guide for land use, growth, future land use, and zoning maps.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Future development should be checked against the updated Comprehensive Plan and Future Land Use/Zoning Map work.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
The plan update materials reference farmland planning issues, including Ag-1 zoning and farmland preservation implications.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Montello
- Westfield
- Oxford
- Packwaukee
- Highway 23 / I-39 access areas

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity uses are most likely around Montello, Westfield, and highway-access communities.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-39
- Highway 23
- Highway 22
- Highway 82

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Current zoning, updated land use code, and future land use/zoning map changes should all be checked before parcel screening.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near communities and I-39/Highway 23 nodes |
| Agricultural Preservation | Check farmland plan update and zoning changes |
| Housing Variety | Verify new residential growth areas in updated plan |
| Economic Growth | Corridor commercial/industrial nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Wetlands, lakes, floodplain, and rural resources |

**Bottom Line**  
Marquette County is important because it is actively updating its comprehensive plan and future land use/zoning map. The update process may reveal emerging growth zones.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Montello, Westfield, and I-39/Highway 23 nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Selected corridor communities.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural/lake towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Montello and Westfield should be checked for infill plus mapped edge growth.

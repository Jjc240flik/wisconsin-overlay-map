## General Breakdown: La Crosse County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on La Crosse County’s Envision 2050 comprehensive plan resources, the county’s future development vision ties land use, housing, transportation, economic development, farmland preservation, and natural resource policy together.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be guided by Envision 2050 and focused around existing communities, infrastructure, and sustainable development areas.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Farmland preservation is explicitly part of the county’s long-term planning framework and should be checked against future land use maps.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- La Crosse
- Onalaska
- Holmen
- West Salem
- Bangor
- Highway / river corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Growth around La Crosse, Onalaska, and Holmen should be checked for infill, redevelopment, and higher-density housing opportunities.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-90
- US Highway 53
- Highway 16
- Mississippi River / metro La Crosse corridor

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
La Crosse County has planning and zoning functions, but municipal plans and zoning maps must be verified for parcel-level use.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth around existing urban services and transportation corridors |
| Agricultural Preservation | Farmland preservation is part of Envision 2050 |
| Housing Variety | Housing needs and infill opportunities near urban nodes |
| Economic Growth | Commercial/industrial growth near corridors and metro areas |
| Environmental Protection | Blufflands, river corridors, floodplain, wetlands, and natural resources |

**Bottom Line**  
La Crosse County should be researched as a metro-core and corridor-growth county, with strong attention to farmland preservation and environmental constraints.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Holmen, Onalaska, and metro La Crosse edge areas.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** West Salem, Bangor, and corridor communities.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural towns away from urban services and corridors.

**Built-Out Communities:** La Crosse core should be reviewed for infill and redevelopment.

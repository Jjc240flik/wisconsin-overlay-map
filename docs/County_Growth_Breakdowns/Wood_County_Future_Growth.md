# Wood County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / future land use map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Wood County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Wood County comprehensive planning resources and related town-level comp plans, the county planning & zoning department provides comprehensive plan guidance and the Future Land Use Plan Map.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be guided by the comprehensive plan, future land use plan maps, and zoning consistency reviews.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Wood County has significant agricultural and rural land, particularly in its eastern and southern portions.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Wisconsin Rapids
- Marshfield
- Nekoosa
- Port Edwards
- Pittsville
- Highway 13 / 73 / 54 / I-39 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity development is most likely near Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, and highway/corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 13
- I-39
- State Highway 73
- State Highway 54

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County planning & zoning provides the framework; town/city/village comprehensive plans and zoning maps must be checked for parcel-level decisions.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, and corridor nodes |
| Agricultural Preservation | Check comprehensive plan and FLU maps |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential expansion near communities |
| Economic Growth | Corridor and community-serving commercial/industrial |
| Environmental Protection | Wisconsin River, wetlands, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Wood County should be researched as a Wisconsin Rapids/Marshfield + I-39 corridor county with strongest targets near existing service centers and highway nodes.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Wisconsin Rapids, Marshfield, I-39 corridor.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Nekoosa, Port Edwards, selected corridor towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Wisconsin Rapids and Marshfield should be checked for infill and edge growth.

# Manitowoc County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs 20-Year Comprehensive Plan / StoryMap map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Manitowoc County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Manitowoc County’s 20-Year Comprehensive Plan resources, future land use planning distinguishes long-term land use direction from current zoning, with future classifications representing development and preservation themes.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Future development should be evaluated against the county’s 20-year land use plan and municipal comprehensive plans.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
The plan’s future land use classifications should be used to identify which agricultural areas are intended to remain rural versus transition to development.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Manitowoc
- Two Rivers
- Kiel
- Valders
- Reedsville
- Highway 10 / I-43 corridor areas

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity uses should be checked near Manitowoc, Two Rivers, Kiel, and other serviced communities.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-43
- US Highway 10
- Highway 151
- Highway 42

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The county plan looks at future land use while zoning addresses current uses; both must be compared for parcel targeting.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near existing cities/villages and service corridors |
| Agricultural Preservation | Distinguish long-term ag preservation from future development areas |
| Housing Variety | Verify future residential/mixed-use plan categories |
| Economic Growth | Identify commercial/industrial nodes and corridors |
| Environmental Protection | Shoreland, wetlands, floodplain, and rural resource constraints |

**Bottom Line**  
Manitowoc County is strong for current-vs-future zoning comparison because the plan explicitly distinguishes future land use from present-day zoning.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Manitowoc, Two Rivers, and I-43 / Highway 10 corridor nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Kiel, Valders, selected serviced villages.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns away from corridors/services.

**Built-Out Communities:** Manitowoc and Two Rivers should be checked for infill/redevelopment.

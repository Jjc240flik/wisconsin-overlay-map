# Menominee County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs Menominee County/Town comprehensive plan map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Menominee County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the County/Town of Menominee Comprehensive Plan 2030 and land use planning resources, future development should be understood through a unified county/town planning context with strong cultural, environmental, and land stewardship considerations.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be carefully guided by the County/Town comprehensive plan, official mapping, and land stewardship objectives.

### 2. Protect Agricultural / Forest / Tribal Land
Menominee County is unique because land stewardship, forest resources, tribal governance, and environmental protection are central to land use decisions.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Keshena
- Neopit
- Zoar / community nodes
- Highway 47 / 55 corridors

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Development should be expected mainly near existing settlements and service areas, not scattered throughout forest/resource lands.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 47
- Highway 55
- Highway VV / local community connectors

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Menominee County/Town planning has a distinct governance structure. Official maps and comprehensive plan consistency are especially important.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near existing communities and services |
| Forest / Resource Preservation | Protect forest, cultural, and environmental resources |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned housing near community nodes |
| Economic Growth | Limited and community-serving commercial development |
| Environmental Protection | Forest, water, wetlands, cultural resources, and sensitive lands |

**Bottom Line**  
Menominee County is not a typical suburban expansion market. Future development opportunities should be evaluated around community nodes while respecting forest, tribal, and environmental land stewardship priorities.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Keshena and Neopit community nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Selected highway/service nodes if mapped.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Forest/resource lands across most of the county.

**Built-Out Communities:** Keshena/Neopit should be reviewed for infill and community-serving growth.

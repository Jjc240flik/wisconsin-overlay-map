# Langlade County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan amendment / farmland preservation map review.

## General Breakdown: Langlade County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Langlade County land records/regulations resources, the county’s future development research should focus on comprehensive plan amendments, farmland preservation mapping, zoning/GIS layers, and town-level plans.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Future development should be guided by the comprehensive plan and zoning map consistency rather than scattered rural conversion.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Langlade County’s farmland preservation map amendments indicate that agricultural preservation is a significant planning issue.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Antigo
- White Lake
- Elcho
- Highway 45 / 64 corridors

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is most likely near Antigo and existing service nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 45
- Highway 64
- Highway 47
- Highway 55

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Langlade County GIS includes parcels, taxes, soils, zoning, and contours; final conclusions require zoning map plus future land use map review.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Antigo and existing serviced nodes |
| Agricultural Preservation | Check farmland preservation map and zoning consistency |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential expansion areas |
| Economic Growth | Identify highway commercial/industrial nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Forest, wetlands, shoreland, and rural resource constraints |

**Bottom Line**  
Langlade County should be researched as a low-to-moderate growth county with Antigo as the primary node and farmland/forest preservation as a major screening factor.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Antigo and highway-adjacent service areas.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Select community nodes such as Elcho/White Lake if mapped.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural/farmland/forest towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Antigo should be checked for infill/redevelopment and mapped edge growth.

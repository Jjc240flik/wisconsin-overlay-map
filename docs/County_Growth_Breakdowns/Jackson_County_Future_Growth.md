# Jackson County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft from official comprehensive plan leads. Needs detailed map/page extraction.

## General Breakdown: Jackson County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the Jackson County Comprehensive Plan and land information planning references, future growth should be coordinated through the Land Use Element, county zoning, and municipal comprehensive plan maps.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Jackson County planning materials emphasize guiding future development and redevelopment of private and public properties with land use maps and plan policies.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Agricultural and rural land protection must be checked against future land use designations and county zoning districts.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Black River Falls
- Hixton
- Merrillan
- Alma Center
- Highway 12 / I-94 access areas

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development should be checked near incorporated communities and existing infrastructure rather than rural forest/agricultural areas.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- I-94
- US Highway 12
- Highway 54
- Highway 27

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Jackson County has county planning/zoning resources, but municipal comprehensive plans and local maps must be checked for parcel-level conclusions.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Black River Falls and serviced communities |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect productive farmland and rural character |
| Housing Variety | Verify residential expansion areas in comp plan maps |
| Economic Growth | Corridor development near I-94 / US 12 nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Forest, wetlands, floodplain, and river corridors require screening |

**Bottom Line**  
Jackson County should be researched as a corridor-and-node county. Best targets are likely around Black River Falls and major highway access areas where future land use maps support development.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Black River Falls and I-94 / US 12 corridor nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Hixton and select highway-access towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural/forest areas away from service nodes.

**Built-Out Communities:** Black River Falls should be reviewed for infill and edge expansion.

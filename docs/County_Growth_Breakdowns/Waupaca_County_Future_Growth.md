# Waupaca County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs adopted future land use map / town comp plans extraction.

## General Breakdown: Waupaca County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Waupaca County planning resources, every town in the county has a comprehensive plan including a Preferred Land Use Map. The county maintains a dedicated Adopted Future Land Use Maps page.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
The future land use map brings together natural resources, economic development, housing, and transportation elements of the comprehensive plan.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Waupaca County has significant agricultural and rural land. Town-level preferred land use maps identify growth vs preservation areas.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Waupaca
- Clintonville (partial)
- New London (partial)
- Weyauwega
- Fremont
- Highway 10 / 45 / 54 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity growth is most likely near Waupaca, New London, and highway corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 10
- State Highway 45
- State Highway 54
- State Highway 110

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Adopted future land use maps and town comprehensive plans are the primary tools. Municipal zoning also applies.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Waupaca, New London, and Highway 10 corridor |
| Agricultural Preservation | Town-level preferred land use maps identify growth areas |
| Housing Variety | Verify residential expansion in preferred land use maps |
| Economic Growth | Corridor commercial/industrial nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Waupaca chain of lakes, wetlands, river corridors, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Waupaca County is excellent for this research because each town has a Preferred Land Use Map. Best targets are near Waupaca, New London, and Highway 10 corridor.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Waupaca, New London, Highway 10 corridor.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Weyauwega, Fremont, selected corridor towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns.

**Built-Out Communities:** Waupaca and New London should be checked for infill and edge growth.

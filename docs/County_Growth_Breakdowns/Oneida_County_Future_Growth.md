# Oneida County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Oneida County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the Oneida County Comprehensive Plan 2025 and planning/zoning resources, the county maintains a comprehensive plan and references local plans for future land use and zoning decisions.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
New development and county policies should focus on existing and planned service areas rather than scattered rural conversion.

### 2. Protect Forest / Agricultural / Lake Resource Land
Oneida County has substantial forest, lake, shoreland, and rural resource considerations. The county forest comprehensive land use plan is also relevant.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Rhinelander
- Minocqua
- Woodruff
- Eagle River (Vilas County edge)
- Highway 47 / 51 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity development is most likely near Rhinelander, Minocqua, and highway corridor service nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 51
- Highway 47
- Highway 70
- Highway 8

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Oneida County Planning and Zoning administers laws affecting landowners. Local plans and county comprehensive plans both matter.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Rhinelander and corridor service communities |
| Forest / Lake / Resource Preservation | Protect forest, lake, shoreland, and rural resource lands |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential areas near communities |
| Economic Growth | Tourism, recreation, and community-serving commercial nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Lakes, shoreland, forests, wetlands, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Oneida County should be researched as a lake/forest tourism and service-node county. Best targets are near Rhinelander, Minocqua, and highway corridor communities.

**Low Growth / Forest Preservation Areas:** Forest/rural resource areas.

**Built-Out Communities:** Rhinelander should be checked for redevelopment/infill.

# Chippewa County – Future Property Development Vision

## General Breakdown: Chippewa County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Chippewa County’s planning documents and future land use information, here’s a clear summary of what Chippewa County planners want to see in future development:

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Chippewa County wants to avoid sprawling, low-density development. New growth should be focused in areas that already have (or are planned to have) public sewer and water service. They prefer infill and redevelopment over constantly expanding into farmland.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
A major goal is to preserve productive farmland where possible. Future Land Use plans show many areas remaining as Agricultural long-term. Growth should be directed to areas that are less suitable for farming.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Most future residential and commercial growth is planned around:
- City of Chippewa Falls
- City of Eau Claire (Chippewa County portion)
- Villages with access to major transportation corridors

These areas are expected to see the majority of new housing and business development.

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Planners want to see more mixed-use developments (residential + commercial together). They encourage higher density in appropriate locations to make better use of infrastructure.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Future development is expected along key transportation routes such as:
- U.S. Highway 53
- State Highway 29
- State Highway 124

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
Important point: Chippewa County does not control zoning in incorporated areas. All actual zoning decisions are made by the individual cities, villages, and towns. The County’s role is more about coordination and planning guidance for unincorporated areas.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority                    | What Planners Want                                      |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Infrastructure Efficiency  | Develop where sewer, water, and roads already exist     |
| Agricultural Preservation  | Protect good farmland from unnecessary development      |
| Housing Variety            | Allow different housing types (single-family, multi-family, mixed-use) |
| Economic Growth            | Support commercial and industrial development in planned areas |
| Environmental Protection   | Avoid development in wetlands, floodplains, and sensitive areas |

**Bottom Line**  
Chippewa County planners want controlled, efficient growth that protects farmland, uses existing infrastructure, happens mostly around existing population centers, and is coordinated between the County and local municipalities.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:**  
Chippewa Falls and Eau Claire (Chippewa County portion) areas — These are the main areas where new residential and commercial development is expected.

**Moderate Growth Areas:**  
Cadott and Lake Hallie areas.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:**  
Eagle Point, Hallie, Lafayette, and Wheaton towns.

**Built-Out Communities:**  
City of Chippewa Falls and City of Eau Claire (Chippewa County portion) are largely developed, so future growth will mostly be redevelopment and infill.

# Richland County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / future land use map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Richland County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the Richland County Comprehensive Plan 2024–2034, the future land use map acts as a strategic tool to guide zoning decisions, infrastructure investments, and development decisions.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be guided by the updated future land use maps for towns under county zoning, which incorporated input from townships.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Richland County has significant agricultural and rural lands. Farmland preservation is central to planning.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Richland Center
- Muscoda
- Viola
- Lone Rock
- Highway 14 / 60 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity uses should be checked near Richland Center and highway corridor nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- US Highway 14
- State Highway 60
- Highway 80
- Highway 58

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
The county comprehensive plan and updated future land use maps provide the framework, but municipal zoning must be checked for parcel-level decisions.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Richland Center and highway corridor nodes |
| Agricultural Preservation | Use updated FLU maps to identify growth vs preservation |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential expansion areas |
| Economic Growth | Corridor commercial/industrial nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Wisconsin River, wetlands, floodplain, and blufflands |

**Bottom Line**  
Richland County should be researched as an agricultural-corridor county with best targets near Richland Center and US Highway 14 / Highway 60 nodes.

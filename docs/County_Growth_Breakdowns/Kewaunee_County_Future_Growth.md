# Kewaunee County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs official county/municipal plan extraction.

## General Breakdown: Kewaunee County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Kewaunee County future development research should focus on comprehensive plans, Bay-Lake Regional Planning Commission resources, municipal future land use maps, and zoning consistency.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Future growth should be evaluated around existing communities and service areas rather than scattered rural conversion.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Kewaunee County has strong rural/agricultural land use considerations. Development targeting should distinguish farmland preservation areas from future residential/commercial transition areas.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Kewaunee
- Algoma
- Luxemburg
- Casco
- Ahnapee / corridor areas

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is likely near established communities, waterfront/community nodes, and highway corridors.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 42
- Highway 54
- Highway 29 access influence

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County, town, village, and city planning documents must be checked separately. Local future land use maps are critical for parcel targeting.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Focus near Kewaunee, Algoma, Luxemburg, and served areas |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect productive farmland unless mapped for transition |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential areas in local comp plans |
| Economic Growth | Identify commercial/industrial nodes near communities/corridors |
| Environmental Protection | Shoreland, wetlands, floodplain, and lake/river corridors require screening |

**Bottom Line**  
Kewaunee County should be researched as a rural/agricultural county with growth concentrated near Kewaunee, Algoma, Luxemburg, and selected highway/community nodes.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Luxemburg, Kewaunee, and Algoma nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Ahnapee and selected highway/community nodes.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns away from services.

**Built-Out Communities:** Kewaunee and Algoma should be reviewed for infill/redevelopment.

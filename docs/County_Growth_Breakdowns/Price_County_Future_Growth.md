# Price County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / forest land use plan map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Price County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Price County zoning and forest comprehensive land use plan resources, Price County is dominated by forest, rural, and natural resource management rather than intensive urban expansion.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Future development should be evaluated near established communities, corridor nodes, and areas outside forest/rural preservation zones.

### 2. Protect Forest / Agricultural Land
More than 92,000 acres of Price County Forest are managed under a comprehensive land use plan through 2035. Forest, rural, and agricultural preservation is central.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Phillips
- Park Falls
- Prentice
- Catawba
- Highway 13 / 111 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is most likely near Phillips, Park Falls, and corridor communities.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 13
- Highway 111
- Highway 70

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County zoning, forest plans, shoreland regulations, and municipal plans all apply. Parcel-level conclusions require multiple layer checks.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Phillips, Park Falls, and corridor nodes |
| Forest / Resource Preservation | Protect county forest land and rural resources |
| Housing Variety | Limited; verify near existing communities |
| Economic Growth | Forest/tourism and community-serving nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Forests, lakes, shoreland, wetlands, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Price County is a low-growth, forest-and-resource-preservation county. Development opportunities are limited to existing communities and corridor nodes.

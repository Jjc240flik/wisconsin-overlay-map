# Polk County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs comprehensive plan / FLU 2030 map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Polk County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Polk County comprehensive plan resources and future land use mapping, the county has established land use categories and a Future Land Use 2030 ArcGIS map.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Future land use maps should be used to promote appropriate distribution of land uses regarding existing and planned infrastructure.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Polk County has substantial agricultural and rural resource considerations, and farmland preservation should be checked against the comprehensive plan.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Amery
- St. Croix Falls
- Osceola
- Frederic
- Luck
- Highway 8 / 35 / 48 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
More intensive development is likely near Amery, St. Croix Falls, and highway corridor service communities.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 8
- Highway 35
- Highway 48
- Highway 243

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County comprehensive plan and FLU map provide the framework, but municipal zoning must be checked for parcel-level accuracy.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near existing communities and corridor nodes |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect productive farmland and rural resource lands |
| Housing Variety | Verify residential expansion in FLU map |
| Economic Growth | Corridor commercial/industrial and tourism nodes |
| Environmental Protection | Lakes, St. Croix River corridor, shoreland, wetlands, and rural resources |

**Bottom Line**  
Polk County should be researched as a Twin Cities-adjacent / lake corridor county with best targets near Amery, St. Croix Falls, Osceola, and highway corridor communities.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Amery, St. Croix Falls, Osceola, Highway 8/35 corridor nodes.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Frederic, Luck, selected corridor towns.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural areas.

**Built-Out Communities:** Amery and St. Croix Falls should be checked for infill and edge growth.

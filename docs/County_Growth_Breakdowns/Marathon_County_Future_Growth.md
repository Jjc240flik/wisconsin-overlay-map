# Marathon County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs detailed Comprehensive Plan 2026 / GIS map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Marathon County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on Marathon County’s comprehensive planning resources, the county’s plan is intended to establish a framework to influence future policy decisions and guide future growth and development over the next 10 to 20 years.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be directed through the county comprehensive plan and local future land use maps, with priority on communities and corridors that can support infrastructure.

### 2. Protect Agricultural Land
Marathon County has substantial agricultural and rural land. Future development screening must distinguish farmland preservation areas from mapped growth areas.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Wausau
- Weston
- Rib Mountain
- Kronenwetter
- Mosinee
- Marathon City
- Highway 29 / 51 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity development is most likely near the Wausau metro area and major highway corridors.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 29
- US Highway 51 / I-39
- Highway 52
- Highway 153

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County GIS and planning resources provide a starting point, but city/village/town zoning and future land use maps must be checked for parcel-level accuracy.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Wausau metro and serviced villages/cities |
| Agricultural Preservation | Protect productive farmland outside mapped growth areas |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential and mixed-use expansion |
| Economic Growth | Corridor and business/industrial park growth along Highway 29 / 51 |
| Environmental Protection | Wetlands, floodplains, forests, and water resources |

**Bottom Line**  
Marathon County should be researched as a Wausau-metro-and-corridor growth county, with strongest opportunities near existing services and Highway 29/51 access.

## Summary of Growth Patterns

**Highest Growth Areas:** Weston, Kronenwetter, Rib Mountain, Wausau metro edge.

**Moderate Growth Areas:** Mosinee, Marathon City, corridor villages.

**Low Growth / Agricultural Preservation Areas:** Rural agricultural towns away from corridors.

**Built-Out Communities:** Wausau should be reviewed for redevelopment/infill.

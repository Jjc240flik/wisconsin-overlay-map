# Sawyer County – Future Property Development Vision

> **Research status:** Source-backed draft. Needs updated comprehensive plan map extraction.

## General Breakdown: Sawyer County’s Vision for Future Property Development

Based on the Sawyer County Comprehensive Plan 2021–2041 and related resources, the county plan includes updates to text, statistical data, maps, goals, and implementation strategies, with future land use classification references.

### 1. Growth Should Be Strategic, Not Scattered
Growth should be guided by the comprehensive plan, future land use classifications, and county zoning code consistency.

### 2. Protect Forest / Lake / Agricultural Land
Sawyer County has substantial forest, lake, shoreland, and agricultural resource lands. The forestry 15-year plan is also relevant.

### 3. Concentrate Development Around Existing Urban Areas
Likely primary growth nodes to verify:
- Hayward
- Winter
- Stone Lake
- Highway 27 / 70 / 77 corridor communities

### 4. Support Mixed-Use and Higher-Density Development
Higher-intensity development is most likely near Hayward and corridor service nodes.

### 5. Guide Growth Along Major Corridors
Key corridors to investigate:
- Highway 27
- Highway 70
- Highway 77

### 6. Each Municipality Controls Its Own Zoning
County comprehensive plan and town-level plans (e.g., Town of Hayward) both apply. Town-of-Hayward plans show zoning districts acceptable within future land use categories.

### 7. Key Priorities for Future Development

| Priority | What Planners Want / What To Verify |
|---|---|
| Infrastructure Efficiency | Growth near Hayward, Winter, and corridor nodes |
| Forest / Lake / Resource Preservation | Protect lake, forest, shoreland, and rural resources |
| Housing Variety | Verify planned residential near community nodes |
| Economic Growth | Tourism, recreation, and community-serving commercial |
| Environmental Protection | Lakes, forests, shoreland, wetlands, and rural landscapes |

**Bottom Line**  
Sawyer County should be researched as a lake/forest/tourism county with strongest targets near Hayward and major corridor service nodes.
